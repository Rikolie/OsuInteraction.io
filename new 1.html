About osu!

osu! is a free-to-play rhythm game developed by Dean "peppy" Herbert. It was initially released for Microsoft Windows in 2007, and has since gained a large and dedicated community of players worldwide.

In osu!, players interact with circles, sliders, and spinners on the screen in time with the rhythm of a chosen song. The game offers various game modes, including standard, taiko, catch the beat, and osu!mania.

One of the key features of osu! is its user-generated content. Players can create and share their own beatmaps, which are custom levels created by placing hit objects to match the beats of a song. This allows for virtually limitless gameplay possibilities.
